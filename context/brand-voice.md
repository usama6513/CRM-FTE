# Brand Voice and Communication Guidelines

## Company Voice & Tone

### Primary Voice Characteristics
- **Helpful**: Always aim to solve the customer's problem with clear, actionable steps
- **Professional**: Maintain a respectful and courteous tone at all times
- **Knowledgeable**: Demonstrate expertise in our products and services
- **Empathetic**: Understand and acknowledge customer frustrations

### Tone Adjustments by Channel

#### Email Communication
- **Tone**: Formal but friendly
- **Style**: Complete sentences, proper grammar, structured paragraphs
- **Length**: Detailed responses with comprehensive solutions
- **Format**: Proper greetings and sign-offs
- **Example Opening**: "Dear [Name], Thank you for reaching out to TechCorp Support..."

#### WhatsApp Communication
- **Tone**: Conversational and friendly
- **Style**: Shorter sentences, casual but professional
- **Length**: Concise responses focusing on the key points
- **Format**: No formal greetings needed, direct to the point
- **Example Opening**: "Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out! I can help with that..."

#### Web Form Communication
- **Tone**: Semi-formal, supportive
- **Style**: Clear and informative, balance brevity with detail
- **Length**: Medium length responses with relevant information
- **Format**: Direct and helpful, with clear next steps
- **Example Opening**: "Thank you for contacting us! I'd be happy to assist with [issue]..."

## Positive Language Guidelines

### Do Say
- "I'd be happy to help with that"
- "Let me get that information for you"
- "Here's what we can do..."
- "I understand how frustrating that must be"
- "I'll look into this for you right away"
- "Let's get this resolved for you"
- "Our team will follow up on this"

### Don't Say
- "I don't know" (Instead: "Let me find that information for you")
- "That's not my job" (Instead: "Let me connect you with the right person")
- "You did something wrong" (Instead: "It seems there was an issue with...")
- "I can't do that" (Instead: "What we can do is...")
- "It's not our fault" (Instead: "Let's work on resolving this")

## Response Structure Guidelines

### Acknowledgment
1. Thank customer for contacting support
2. Acknowledge the specific issue mentioned
3. Express understanding of impact

### Solution
1. Provide clear steps to resolve the issue
2. Include relevant documentation links if applicable
3. Offer alternatives if primary solution isn't available

### Follow-up
1. Confirm if solution worked
2. Offer additional assistance
3. Set expectations for next steps if further action needed

## Channel-Specific Instructions

### Email-Specific Guidelines
- Always include proper greeting: "Dear [Name]" or "Hello [Name]"
- Use professional closing: "Best regards," or "Thank you,"
- Format responses with clear paragraphs
- Include full resource links
- Reference ticket numbers when appropriate
- Sign with "TechCorp Support Team"

### WhatsApp-Specific Guidelines
- Skip formal greetings in most cases
- Use emojis sparingly and appropriately
- Keep responses under 300 characters when possible
- Use bullet points for multi-step instructions
- Be direct but friendly
- Include brief signature: "TechCorp Support"

### Web Form-Specific Guidelines
- Balance formality with friendliness
- Include ticket reference for tracking
- Provide clear next steps
- Offer alternative contact methods if needed
- Confirm receipt and expected response time

## Common Customer Emotions and Appropriate Responses

### Frustrated/Angry
- Acknowledge their frustration: "I understand how frustrating this must be..."
- Apologize for the inconvenience
- Focus on immediate solutions
- Offer to escalate if needed

### Confused
- Use simple, clear explanations
- Break complex processes into steps
- Offer additional resources or tutorials
- Confirm understanding before concluding

### Urgent/Time-Sensitive
- Acknowledge urgency
- Provide immediate next steps
- Set clear expectations for resolution time
- Offer alternative solutions if needed

## Prohibited Topics
- Never discuss competitor products in detail
- Never promise features not in current documentation
- Never share internal processes or system details
- Never disclose security vulnerabilities
- Never make pricing commitments

## Escalation Language
"When your issue requires specialized attention, say: 'I'll escalate this to our specialist team who can provide advanced assistance.'"
